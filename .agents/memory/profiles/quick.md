- ## Photosynthesis
- Photosynthesis is the process used by plants, algae, and some bacteria to convert light energy into chemical energy, in the form of glucose (sugar). This chemical energy then fuels the organism's activities.
- ### Key Components:
- Light Energy:** Typically from the sun.
- Carbon Dioxide (CO₂):** Absorbed from the atmosphere.
- Water (H₂O):** Absorbed from the soil.
- Chlorophyll:** A green pigment found in chloroplasts, responsible for absorbing light energy.
- ### Process Summary:
- 1.  Light energy is captured by chlorophyll.
- 2.  Water molecules are split, releasing oxygen (O₂), protons, and electrons.
- 3.  Carbon dioxide is converted into glucose using the captured energy and the products from water splitting.
- ### Overall Chemical Equation:
- 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ (glucose) + 6O₂
- ### Significance:
- Oxygen Production:** Releases oxygen into the atmosphere, essential for aerobic life.
- Food Source:** Forms the base of most food chains on Earth.
- Energy Conversion:** Converts radiant energy into chemical energy, sustaining life.
